Zhipu's GLM-5.1 Becomes First Open-Weight Model to Top SWE-Bench Pro

Enterprises looking for frontier-grade reasoning without API dependencies now have an on-premise option; geopolitically, it marks a real decoupling of the global AI supply chain. Watch for Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google benchmark counter-releases over the next two weeks.

NASA Greenlights Nuclear-Powered Mars Scout for 2028 Launch

NASA announced formal development of Space Reactor-1 "Freedom," a 20+ kW fission-powered spacecraft targeted for a December 2028 launch to Mars. The probe will carry three Ingenuity-class helicopters to scout landing sites and search for subsurface water, and it represents the first serious fielding of nuclear fission for interplanetary propulsion since the concept was studied and shelved in the 1960s. Nuclear electric propulsion cuts Mars transit time from 6–9 months to roughly three, which is the threshold at which crewed missions become medically feasible. The White House has separately directed an accelerated timeline on a lunar-surface reactor, suggesting U.S. space policy is committing in earnest to nuclear-in-space as the dominant power architecture for the 2030s.

Google Reveals TurboQuant, Cutting KV-Cache Memory by Orders of Magnitude

A Google Research paper presented at ICLR 2026 introduces TurboQuant, an efficiency technique that pairs PolarQuant vector rotation with Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression to shrink the KV cache — one of the largest memory costs in long-context LLM inference. Early benchmarks suggest 200K+ token contexts can run at a fraction of current GPU memory footprints with minimal quality degradation.
