The Compute Gold Rush Redefines The AI Stack

Signals: OpenAI Commits $20B To Cerebras Over Three Years · Nvidia Vera Rubin In Full Production On TSMC 3nm · TSMC Q1 Revenue Up 35% On 80% CoWoS Packaging Growth · Meta Raises Quest Prices As Memory Chips Rerouted To AI Data Centers · Anthropic Hits $30B ARR Spending 4× Less Than OpenAI On Training

The AI industry is reorganizing around compute scarcity rather than model capability. OpenAI's tripled Cerebras commitment, Nvidia's Rubin platform locking up TSMC's advanced packaging, and Meta passing AI memory-cost inflation onto VR consumers all point in one direction: the binding constraint is silicon, power, and data-center siting — not research talent. Meanwhile Anthropic's disclosed $30B ARR at roughly a quarter of OpenAI's training spend shows efficiency can still win margin, even as hyperscalers race to lock in capacity.

Top Opportunity

Enterprises signing multi-year AI infrastructure contracts should index pricing and SLAs to compute-supply conditions, not headline model versions. The downstream repricing risk is larger than most procurement teams have modeled — and power-adjacent real estate, cooling, and grid-interconnect vendors are now the real bottleneck to watch.

The IMF's spring package delivers a split-screen message: commodity-exporters gain, importers bleed, and the real risk is not the Middle East shock itself but what leverage and passive flows do on the way through. Carry-trade unwinds, tight credit spreads, and concentrated equity positioning mean a second shock — or even a reversal of last week's oil relief rally — could cascade through nonbank balance sheets. This is the moment to negotiate contingency funding lines, not after a margin call.
