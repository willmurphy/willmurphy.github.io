Google Dismantles GridTide: China-Linked Espionage Across 42 Countries

Google's Threat Intelligence Group and Mandiant disclosed the disruption of UNC2814, a Chinese state-linked cyberespionage campaign that compromised 53 organizations in 42 countries using a novel backdoor called GridTide. The malware's innovation was its use of Google Sheets as a command-and-control channel — commands were passed through cell A1 while exfiltrated data flowed through adjacent cells, making the traffic nearly invisible to traditional network monitoring. Targeted data included personal identifying information such as voter IDs and national identity numbers, suggesting the operation's goal was surveillance of individuals rather than intellectual property theft. Google responded by sinkholing associated domains, terminating compromised Cloud accounts, and disabling the Sheets instances. The operation had been active since 2017, underscoring how long sophisticated state actors can persist undetected. This disclosure is likely to accelerate corporate adoption of zero-trust architectures and API-level monitoring for SaaS platforms.

Ai2 Releases Olmo Hybrid: Efficiency Over Scale

The Allen Institute for AI (Ai2) released Olmo Hybrid on March 6, a 7-billion-parameter open model that combines transformer attention layers with linear recurrent layers to achieve what the team claims is 2× data efficiency compared to standard transformer architectures. The hybrid approach allows the model to process long sequences more efficiently by using recurrent layers for routine pattern matching while reserving full attention for complex reasoning tasks.
