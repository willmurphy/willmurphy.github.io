The Global Economy in the Shadow of War

Signals: IMF Cuts 2026 Growth To 3.1% · Global Inflation Revised Up To 4.4% · Strait Of Hormuz Oil Shock · EM Commodity Importers Under Pressure · US Consumer Sentiment Falls To 50.0

The IMF's spring Outlook — titled "Global Economy in the Shadow of War" — marks the clearest break yet from the post-COVID disinflation story. Growth has been downgraded, inflation is ticking back up, and the pain is asymmetric: oil-importing emerging markets and indebted mid-income economies face the steepest squeeze while net energy exporters get a temporary lift. Central banks are now likely to hold rates higher for longer than markets priced just a quarter ago.

Top Opportunity

Treasurers and CFOs should re-stress-test 2026 plans against a "higher-for-longer plus oil shock" scenario; hedging fuel, reshoring optionality, and dollar-funded EM exposure deserve fresh scrutiny this quarter.

Insight

The Quiet Social Exit

Signals: Pew — 49% Of Americans Get News By Chance · Pew — Teens' Nuanced, Mostly Positive Platform Experience · "Chaos Culture" Replacing Polished Feeds · "Social Exit" To Newsletters And Podcasts

The cultural story isn't panic about screens — it's fatigue with performance. New Pew data shows both that most teens now describe social media as mostly positive and nuanced, and that half of US adults have stopped actively seeking news at all. Audiences are drifting from algorithmic feeds toward newsletters, podcasts, and closer niches, while creators lean into raw "chaos culture" over polished output. The scrolling era isn't ending with a crash — it's ending with a shrug.

Top Opportunity

Brands and publishers optimizing for impressions should shift measurement toward owned, opt-in channels (email, RSS, private communities) where the "exiting" audience is reconcentrating and CPMs are not yet priced for scarcity.
