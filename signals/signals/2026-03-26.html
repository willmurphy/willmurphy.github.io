EU Cyber Resilience Act: Open Source Enters the Compliance Countdown

With the EU Cyber Resilience Act's vulnerability-reporting requirements set to kick in on September 11, 2026, software companies worldwide are racing to understand what compliance means when their products incorporate open-source components. The CRA places full legal liability for embedded open-source code on the manufacturer that ships the final product — not the upstream community developer — and requires detailed Software Bills of Materials (SBOMs). Non-compliance risks fines up to €15 million or 2.5% of global turnover. Community-maintained, non-commercialized projects are exempt, but the boundary between "community" and "commercial" is actively contested, particularly in the GitHub ecosystem.
