IEEPA Tariffs Struck Down, Replaced by Section 122 Authority

The Supreme Court ruled 6-3 on February 20 that the International Emergency Economic Powers Act does not authorize tariffs, invalidating the administration's primary tariff mechanism. The White House responded by invoking Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974, which permits tariffs of up to 15% on all imported goods for up to 150 days to address trade imbalances — keeping effective rates elevated. The Budget Lab at Yale estimates all 2025 tariffs reduced long-run GDP by a persistent 0.6%, with real GDP growth 0.9 percentage points lower in calendar 2025. Today, April 2, marks one year since the original "Liberation Day" tariff announcement.
