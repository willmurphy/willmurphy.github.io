📈 Economic

UAE To Quit OPEC And OPEC+ On May 1

The UAE will exit OPEC after nearly 60 years of membership, ending a quota dispute with Saudi Arabia and freeing ADNOC to push toward 5 million barrels per day by 2027. The departure cuts OPEC's share of global supply from roughly 30% to 26% and removes one of the few members with meaningful spare capacity, weakening the cartel's ability to defend prices on the way down or shock-absorb on the way up. With 9.1 million bpd already shut in across the Gulf because of the Hormuz crisis, the move is symbolically larger than its near-term volume impact.
