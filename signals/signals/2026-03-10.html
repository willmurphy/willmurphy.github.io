Trump's suggestion that the U.S. could "take over" the Strait of Hormuz briefly calmed markets before being dismissed as impractical, underscoring the degree to which energy pricing is now driven by geopolitical rhetoric as much as supply fundamentals.

A real-time energy risk management platform targeting mid-market logistics, manufacturing, and food companies — offering scenario modeling for supply chain re-routing around conflict zones, automated hedging triggers calibrated to geopolitical event feeds, and cost pass-through calculators — would address an acute need that intensifies with every day the Hormuz disruption persists.
