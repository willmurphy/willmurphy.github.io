The "Pick-and-Shovel" Layer Is Where 2026 AI Money Is Going

The latest wave of AI-cycle capital is no longer flowing into model-shop rounds — it is flowing into the substrate that runs them. KKR's new Helix vehicle wraps power generation, transmission and data-center build into a single platform; SoftBank's Roze pulls robotics and data-center assets into a public vehicle that could list at $100B; Apple's record quarter and fresh $100B buyback show that the cash-rich players are using strength to buy back stock rather than chase model breakthroughs. Operators with site-control, interconnect rights, transformer supply, or specialty cooling/silicon stand to be the most strategic counterparties of the next 18 months. Vendors selling into this layer should orient roadmaps to the procurement calendars of the new infra mega-vehicles, not the labs.

The Iran-War Energy Scar Is Outlasting the Ceasefire

Signals: Brent Holds ~$110–$118 Three Weeks Past April 8 Ceasefire · Hormuz Traffic Still Far Below Pre-War Levels · World Bank Sees +24% Energy Prices In 2026 · IMF Cuts Global Growth To 3.1%

The shooting war ended on April 8, but the supply-side disruption is proving sticky. Tanker insurance, GCC infrastructure damage and slow re-flagging mean that even Iran's offer to fully reopen the strait has not pulled barrels back to pre-war flow. The IMF and World Bank are now baking that persistence into their base cases, which is what is keeping the Fed on hold and emerging-market importers under stress. The macro story for Q2 and Q3 is no longer "will the war end?" but "what does a $100+ Brent regime feel like once it stops being a shock and becomes a level?"

Top Opportunity

Plans built on H1 rate cuts and sub-$90 oil need to be re-underwritten this month. Procurement, freight contracts and FX hedges in EM-importer geographies are the most mispriced — there is still alpha in re-pricing them before consensus catches up.
